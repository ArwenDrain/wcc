Lise Meitner

"You must not blame scientists for the use to which war technicians have put our discoveries."

Lise Meitner, an Austrian physicist, collabortated with Otto Hahn in their work on radioactive elements and nuclear physics.When Hahn discovered that uranium atoms were split when bombarded with neutrons, Meitner then calculated the energy released in the reaction and named the phenomenon "nuclear fission." This discovery would eventually lead to the atomic bomb.

Interesting Facts

  1. Meitner was forced to flee Nazi Germany by 1939.
  2. Otto Hahn was awarded the Nobel Prize in 1944 for the discovery of the fission of heavy nuclei, however, Meitner was overlooked by the Nobel committee.
  3. Meitner was invited to work on the Manhattan project at Los Alamos, but she declined.
